Projecting Baylor's Offensive Depth Chart: Backup QB Battle & Solidified Skill Positions?

Author By SicEm365 Staff
August 19, 2025
6
1,436
Tweet Share Jump to Discussion

As the Bears inch closer to their season opener against Auburn on Friday, Aug. 29, at McLane Stadium, Grayson Grundhoefer and Levi Caraway try to predict Baylor’s depth chart, starting with the offense.

Quarterback

  • Sawyer Robertson (RSr.) / Nate Bennett (RFr.) / Walker White (RFr.) / Edward Griffin (Fr.)

Sawyer Robertson heads into his senior campaign as the unquestioned starter, while Coppell High School standout Edward Griffin likely redshirts and slots into the fourth-string spot. In between, however, is a peculiar battle for the backup role between Nate Bennett and Auburn transfer Walker White, with Bennett having the upper hand throughout fall camp.

The expectation from the fanbase is that White, the former four-star recruit, would be handed the reins following Robertson’s eventual departure. However, in the meantime, as White still looks to put it all together and understand Jake Spavital’s offensive scheme, the Bears will likely turn toward Bennett if they’re in a pinch, especially early in the season.

Running Back

  • Bryson Washington (RSo.) / Caden Knighten (Fr.) / Michael Turner (Fr.) OR Joseph Dodds (RFr.)

With the season-ending foot injury to Dawson Pendergrass, Baylor went from having two of the top running backs in the Big 12 to being forced to rely on at least one of the three four-star freshman running backs — Caden Knighten, Joseph Dodds and Michael Turner — to play a significant role.

The good news is that all signs point to Knighten, who has received nothing but praise from Aranda and other teammates, being ready for that challenge. Knighten and Washington could be a formidable one-two punch, but both will need to stay healthy because if not, things could get hairy. Dodds is coming off an Achilles injury, and Turner is fresh off a meniscus injury.

Wide Receiver

  • WR: Josh Cameron (RSr.) / Jadon Porter (So.)
  • Slot: Ashtyn Hawkins (6Sr.) / Kobe Prentice (Sr.)
  • WR: Louis Brown IV (RSr.) OR Kole Wilson (Sr.)

This is as clean as it’s going to get for the position at the moment. These are the six guys I expect to have a major role all season long if they are healthy. I have them listed by the positions where they have been getting the most playing time, but it's also important to note that Ashtyn Hawkins, Kole Wilson, Kobe Prentice and Jadon Porter have played in the slot and outside.

Louis Brown IV and Josh Cameron have been almost exclusively on the outside. I expect a lot of rotation with this group to ensure players are fresh when they are on the field. This is a very exciting group of six with the potential to be one of the best in the Big 12.

There are too many players to name who could have a role, but it will likely come down to who ends up redshirting. Here is my ranking of redshirt candidates from most likely to play this season to less likely: Taz Williams, Jacorey Watson, Chase Collier, Michael Johnson and Ashton Jones.

Aside from that group, you have Mason Dossett (RFr.) and Purdue transfer Ryne Shackelford (RSo.), who could see roles as young and developing prospects to get more speed on the field. The last name is Southern transfer Tyler Kirkwood (RSr.), who could eat up another depth spot from a true freshman this season.

Tight End

  • Michael Trigg (RSr.) / Kelsey Johnson (RJr.) OR Matthew Klopfenstein (Jr.)

Baylor’s talented tight end Michael Trigg will more than likely be playing on Sundays in 2026, so as long as he stays healthy, he’ll see the lion’s share of reps at tight end. But as we saw last year, Spavital used Gavin Yates a ton, mostly as a lead blocker in the run game, but occasionally as a pass catcher as well.

It’ll be interesting to see whether Kelsey Johnson, who missed all of last year with an injury, or Matthew Klopfenstein, who caught three passes in place of an injured Trigg against LSU, will slide into that Yates-type role, but our money’s on Johnson. The 6-foot-3, 248-pound prospect made an instant impact as a freshman in 2022, including a two-touchdown performance against No. 4 TCU, and played in all 12 games in 2023.

Offensive Line

  • RT: Kaden Sieracki (RJr.) / Joe Crocker (RSo.) / Matthew Parker (Fr.)
  • RG: Omar Aigbedion (Sr.) / Harrison Cluff (Fr.) / Connor Cameron (RFr.)
  • C: Coleton Price (RJr.) / Koltin Sieracki (RFr.) / Wes Tucker (RSo.)
  • LG: Ryan Lengyel (RSr.) / Colton Thomasson (RSo.) / Isaiah Robinson (RSo.)
  • LT: Sidney Fugar (RSr.) / Sean Thompkins (RSo.)

The group is loaded with four starters returning on an offensive line that ended the season as one of the best in the conference. The only new starter is at right tackle, where Kaden Sieracki replaces Campbell Barrington.

The good news is Sieracki has 924 snaps under his belt, but the bad news is he had the worst PFF Grade of any role player on Baylor’s roster. He will likely decide whether Baylor takes a big step forward or not, since the rest of the group looks ready to go and has improved. The coaches and players have faith, but we all have to see it, and he will face significant challenges right out of the gate against Auburn.

The two starters I heard the most about this offseason were Omar Aigbedion and Coleton Price. I expect both of them to improve even more from a year ago. I also believe that Sidney Fugar is going to feel much more comfortable in the scheme now after starting at left tackle in the second half of last season.

From a depth perspective, I feel really good about two players: Koltin Sieracki and Sean Thompkins. Both of these players have put together a strong offseason and will be counted on off the bench to help solidify the group. I have risen on Colton Thomasson, Joe Crocker and Harrison Cluff throughout the offseason, but am not entirely sold that there wouldn’t be a drop off from the starter. Aside from that, many guys have potential, but I need to see and hear more before I can gauge their future impact.
