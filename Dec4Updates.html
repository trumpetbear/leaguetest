- As Dave Aranda mentioned in his signing day press conference, Baylor is moving on to staff evaluations and changes. The first domino fell early on Wednesday evening with Inoke Breckterfield making the move to Oregon State to join a staff with a former co-worker at Washington.

- Also as alluded to by Aranda, and as we've been discussing here, the expectation is that Baylor will hire a play calling defensive coordinator. This has been the plan for awhile, and there has some good feedback about the candidates being considered. I wouldn't expect this to drag out too long and Aranda won't have to move too far down his list. A DC will have some say over the defensive staff as well, so that's something to keep an eye on as well. The DC will obviously coach a position of their own.

- Exit interviews are done and early feedback is that there were a lot of tough conversations. The next few days will be worth watching closely. Keep in mind these revenue share deals have always been year to year but things seem to have been a little more cut throat. If a player isn't taking care of business in any off-field area, while also not a contributor all ready, you are in line to be replaced. Said differently, there is going to be a large redistribution of funds with the current roster.

- Baylor will have a large amount of money to spend in the portal because of what I just mentioned and the intentional decision to not match deals for the few high school recruits that moved on at the end of the cycle. Just the difference in payments would have been enough for several impact transfer portal players at minimum. This is the third transfer portal cycle that Baylor has really had funds to utilize (2024, 2025, 2026), and it will be the most Baylor has had and it's not really close.

- Building on that, there is an opportunity for growth in the external NIL department that is being utilized in a lot of CFB right now. If and when this goes into effect, it's very good news for the future of the program, not just in 2026. This is always going to be a moving target and Baylor has to stay on top of things. This will be critical for the new AD to be forward thinking and not reactionary.

Happy Thursday.
