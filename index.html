If you really wanted to get technical, you'd state that sometimes the brass overblows and there is phasing in the marching. I really try to go easy on BUGWB. These guys may get 6 hours of practice per week - if that. Many of the members, of course, are not even music majors.

I don't like the overblowing. That's high school crap and shows a complete disregard for the science of sound. Give me a tuba line that plays in tune and pitch and they will sound essentially like one giant horn. But if you scatter the tune and pitch among 15 trumpet players, it sounds awful because you have all these notes going out of tune or pitch one way or the other. It neither helps the line be loud nor the band sound good as a whole. There are enough musicians in the band that the non-musicians should be able to learn these basics.

Finally, as long as we're getting picky, I hope we get a percussion pit one day so we can have some true percussion pieces. I understand not taking your pit on the road due to costs and the number of instruments you have to haul, but we should have a pit for home games. I love a good pit.

BUGWB just isn't a priority for either the university or the school of music, so it is what it is.

I should add that - while I would love to see several changes - I truly appreciate the students who dedicate their time to BUGWB.

When I taught at Baylor, I saw how much band members contributed and how little benefit they received. They were active, engaged fans in addition to great musicians and students.

Baylor football is one of my favorite things in the world and BUGWB is the only acceptable soundtrack to Saturdays in the fall. Thanks to all who have contributed to that in the past or are currently.

Parent of a former BUGWB member and Music Ed major. Yes, they pay to play. It is a one credit class that we pay tuition for. Music Ed majors are the only ones required to do 2 years. Performance majors are encouraged but not required, I know this because his roommates were performance majors and not in BUGWB. My son did 4.5 years and section leader his senior year. Played at soccer games, basketball games, volleyball games, etc. Small scholarships are only given to non-music majors as an incentive to get them to join and yes they do have MCC students. Not many, but those students also have to take an extra class at MCC to do it and pay a fee.

They do a lot with less and if you ever stayed for 5th quarter to listen to them, you'll appreciate them even more. For an 11:00 game they get there around 7 am and are literally the last ones to leave the field.

They are goofy (they are band kids) but loud and love our team!
